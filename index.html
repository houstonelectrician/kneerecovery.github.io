Am I Ready?

Option C: checklist + decision flow (sport-agnostic)

Readiness Levels (use as “gates”)

Gate 1

Walk: daily walking without increasing swelling next day.

Gate 2

Jog: short jogs with stable swelling/pain pattern over 48 hours.

Gate 3

Run: longer straight-line running, stable next-day knee.

Gate 4

Sprint: short bursts, no “reactive swelling” spike.

Gate 5

Cut: deceleration + direction change with confidence.

Gate 6

Contact: sport-like chaos: bumps, awkward steps, fatigue.

Disclaimer: Educational only; not medical advice; not a substitute for a clinician. Consult your surgeon/PT for diagnosis and treatment. In emergencies, call 911 or go to the ER.

Quick Decision Flow

If swelling is trending up (worse next day), you are not ready to progress a gate.

If pain is sharp/locking or there is true instability/giving way, do not “push through.”

If confidence is the limiter but symptoms are stable, progress with controlled exposures (not hero sessions).

    Pain & Swelling

    Signals, patterns, and when to escalate

    Green-ish

    Predictable soreness that fades with warm-up and does not spike swelling the next day.

    Yellow

    Swelling trend rising over 48 hours, stiffness increasing, pain creeping up with volume.

    Red

    Locking, true giving way, fever/redness, calf pain/swelling, or sudden sharp pain change.

    Disclaimer: Educational only; not medical advice; not a substitute for a clinician. If you have red flags, contact your clinician or urgent care/ER.

    Mindset (Mental Recovery)

    Calm confidence rebuild, not forced optimism

    Common blocks

    • “I’m cleared but I don’t trust it.”
    • Fear spikes when you add chaos (fatigue, contact, uneven ground).
    • Confidence is a skill: progressive exposure beats heroic jumps.
    Disclaimer: Educational only; not medical advice; not a substitute for a clinician.

    A simple exposure ladder

    1. Control: rehearsed movements
    2. Speed: increase pace slightly
    3. Decision: add reaction cues
    4. Chaos: fatigue + unpredictability

    Measure “confidence” like a metric: if symptoms are stable, add one small variable at a time.

    Disclaimer: Educational only; not medical advice; not a substitute for a clinician.

    Train While Injured

    Maintain VO₂, strength, and sanity without inflaming the knee

    Cardio (low knee load)

    Bike / upper-body erg / swimming (as cleared). Keep swelling response as the limiter.

    Strength (safe bias)

    Upper body + trunk + hip dominance. Progress only if next-day knee remains stable.

    Body comp

    Injury phases can add boredom-eating. Simple structure beats willpower.

    Default FAQ pack

    Why being “cleared” doesn’t always mean “ready”

    Medical clearance often focuses on tissue healing and basic function; sport demands require capacity + confidence + chaos tolerance.

    What if swelling keeps coming back?

    Treat swelling trend as your governor: reduce volume/intensity and coordinate with your clinician for specific guidance.

    When should I message my PT/surgeon?

    Any red flags (locking, giving way, fever/redness, calf pain/swelling) or sudden symptom shifts—don’t “wait it out.”

    How do I rebuild confidence?

    Progressive exposure: add one variable at a time (speed → decision → chaos) while keeping symptoms stable.

    Can I keep fitness while injured?

    Often yes—choose modalities that don’t provoke swelling and follow your restrictions.
